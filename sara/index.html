Western Tropical

Vedic Sidereal

In the sidereal chart, the transiting Moon is in Scorpio at 21 degrees, moving through Jyeshtha nakshatra, which is ruled by Mercury and governed by Indra, the god of sovereignty and storm. Jyeshtha carries authority, but it does not carry ease. It is the nakshatra of the elder who has seen too much to be naive, and the Moon here draws out whatever emotional knowledge has been gathered without celebration. For you, with your natal 4th house Moon in Capricorn in Uttara Ashadha, this Scorpio Moon transits your 2nd house, the house of accumulated resource, of voice, of lineage. What you have built, what your voice is worth, and what you inherited without asking for it are all present in today's emotional atmosphere.

The sidereal Sun is in Taurus in Rohini nakshatra, your 8th house. Rohini is ruled by the Moon and presided over by Brahma, the creator. It is one of the most fertile and beauty-saturated nakshatras in the entire zodiac, and its placement in the 8th house today creates an unusual convergence: the creative and generative force is operating in the house of depth, transformation, and what is hidden from ordinary sight. Sarita is working in the unseen layer right now, whether she knows it or not. What is being made may not yet have a name or a form others can see.

Transiting Saturn in Pisces at 18 degrees sits in your 6th house sidereal, making a hard aspect to your natal 4th house Saturn in Capricorn. In Vedic terms this is a Saturn return echo, not the primary return itself but a resonance of it, calling your attention back to discipline, to the daily structures that support the larger work. The farm is not separate from the practice. The soil is not less sacred than the stage. Saturn here asks whether the rhythms of your days are built to sustain a decades-long life of creation, or whether they are being improvised week to week. There is a difference, and you feel it in the body at the end of long tours.

You are in Rahu Mahadasha, likely in a sub-period that has been amplifying themes of expansion, ambiguity, and the cost of visibility. Rahu in your natal 2nd house in Scorpio connects to the transiting Moon today, activating the axis between what you give the world and what you keep for yourself. The hunger that Rahu instills is not meant to be satisfied today. It is meant to be clarified. Ask not what you want more of, but what you want to mean.

Sacred Flame

Blue Flame — El Morya — Will, Protection, Faith, First Ray

The Blue Flame is the first ray, the ray of pure will, and it does not present itself as gentle. El Morya's flame does not soften the thing that needs deciding. It illuminates it. On a day when two full-moon axes are pulling at your chart from multiple directions, the Blue Flame arrives not as comfort but as a spine. The question it asks is simple: what is yours to do, and do you have the courage to do only that? Not everything, not the version of you that serves every room. The specific, irreducible thing.

For Sara, who tends a farm and crosses time zones and keeps paint on her hands and calluses on her fingers, the Blue Flame offers protection not as a shield but as a clarity of purpose. When you know what you are for, other things fall away naturally. The faith this flame asks for is not faith in outcomes. It is faith in the direction itself, in the decision to keep walking toward the work even when the harvest is not yet visible. Plant the note. Plant the seed. Plant the color. Walk forward.
