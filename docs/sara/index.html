Rahu sits in your second house in Scorpio, and its Mahadasha — now well into its middle years — has been reshaping the very substance of your livelihood and your voice. The second house in Jyotish governs accumulated wealth, speech, what you consume and what you offer through your mouth: words, song, sustenance. Rahu here is ravenous and unconventional — it pushes you toward forms of earning and expression that do not fit inherited patterns. The musician who travels, the farmer who paints, the artist who moves between worlds: Rahu in the second house crafts an identity made of gathered contraries, and it is not done with you yet. Today, with transiting planets emphasizing steady resource and responsible love in the Vedic sky, the dasha whispers: consolidate what Rahu has gathered. Do not let the hunger consume what the labor has built.

Mars is debilitated in Libra in your first house, conjunct the Vishakha Ascendant — the nakshatra of the forked path, ruled by Jupiter and Indra together, the star of ambition and patient ripening. A debilitated Mars conjunct the lagna does not mean weakness; it means force that has learned to negotiate. The warrior who paints, the body that carries both hammer and brush — this Mars knows how to enter a room without overpowering it. Today the transiting Scorpio Moon (in sidereal terms) casts its intensity through the second house of resources, activating Rahu's corridor directly. What you feel today about money, voice, worth — feel it, but do not act rashly. Rahu distorts; the Moon amplifies. Wait for the feeling to settle into knowing.

Your natal Moon in Capricorn in the fourth house — Uttara Ashadha nakshatra, ruled by the Sun — gives you a foundation of quiet, unyielding endurance. The roots go deep, the home is a practice, the land itself is your anchor. Sarita performs at the edge of the world, but Sara returns to the earth. Today's Vedic sky, with Venus (your lagna lord) forming a harmonious angle to Saturn, blesses the fourth-house domain: home, land, the inner life. Tend the farm with intention today. The Uttara Ashadha nakshatra asks for the long victory, the one that comes not from speed but from not stopping.
