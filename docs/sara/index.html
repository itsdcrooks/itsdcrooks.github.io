Rahu's Hunger Meets the Grounded Earth

Libra ASC Rahu Dasha Mars ☌ Lagna Capricorn Moon Virgo Sun 12H Rahu 2H Scorpio Saturn 4H Capricorn

Rahu sits in the 2nd house of this Libra-rising chart — Scorpio, the sign of hidden wealth, secret speech, and the voice that carries unseen power. The Mahadasha of Rahu, running through approximately 2028, is the period during which the voice itself becomes the primary karma. Rahu in Scorpio in the 2nd does not ask gently; it insists that resources — material, vocal, and artistic — be accumulated through intensity, through depths others avoid. Today's transiting Moon in Capricorn activates the 4th house, where natal Saturn and Moon already sit in this Vedic chart. The 4th house is the seat of home, inner foundation, and the land itself. The farm is not incidental to the dharma — it is the roots that make the voice sustainable.

Mars is debilitated in Libra in this sidereal chart, conjunct the Lagna — a warrior placed in the diplomat's court. The debilitation is real, but it is mitigated by Mercury's conjunction and by Venus ruling the Ascendant. What it produces is force expressed through aesthetic judgment: the ability to act decisively on questions of beauty, craft, and relationship, while struggling to act purely on aggression alone. Mars cannot bully its way through Libra. It must negotiate, calibrate, and create. Today's transit of the Moon over the natal Saturn-Moon pairing in the 4th brings the body back to its foundation. Before the graha accelerates outward, it must check in with the soil beneath it.

The nakshatra of the transiting Moon today is Uttara Ashadha — the same nakshatra as her natal Moon and natal Saturn. This is a Moon returning to its own resonance field. Uttara Ashadha is ruled by the Sun and governed by the Vishvadevas, the universal deities of collective benefit. The nakshatra carries the quality of final victory through patience rather than speed — it rewards the one who keeps working after the initial energy fades. Sara's dharmic task today is precisely this: sustain the effort when the Mars-node square tempts either surrender or reckless push. Uttara Ashadha says the ground holds. Keep working it.
