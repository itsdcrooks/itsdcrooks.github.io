Rahu moves through your 2nd house in Scorpio — the house of speech, accumulated wealth, and the particular treasure of the artist: the voice as livelihood. Since approximately 2010, this shadow planet has been amplifying everything connected to how you earn, how you speak, how you are known through sound. The 2nd house rules what we possess and what we say; Rahu here hungers for more than ordinary accumulation — it seeks the kind of wealth that cannot be deposited, the kind that accrues in the marrow of listeners. You are in the long arc of making your voice something irreversible in the world. Today, with the transiting Moon moving through Scorpio in the sidereal sky, it activates Rahu's own sign, sharpening this axis: what do you want your voice to carry beyond this life?

Uttara Ashadha's deity is the Vishvadevas — the universal gods, all of them at once — reminding you that your dharma is not singular. You are allowed to be musician and farmer and painter and woman. The debilitated Mars makes you versatile, not diminished.

Your natal Saturn in Capricorn in the 4th house — in its own sign, exalted in its discipline — governs the home, the land, the roots. Saturn is comfortable here, but not soft. Today it holds your Uttara Ashadha Moon in a structural embrace: whatever feeling the day brings, the foundation beneath you was laid carefully. Your farm is not accidental. Your roots abroad are not accidental. Ketu in Taurus in the 8th house holds your past-life treasury of material steadiness, released now in service of Rahu's hunger in the 2nd — you have always known how to sustain yourself, and now the question is what you sustain beyond yourself, what you leave in the field of the world's hearing.
