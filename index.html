What Rahu Desires, Venus Must Transform

Sag ASC Gemini Sun · 7H Libra Moon · 11H Rahu Dasha Venus Antardasha Ardra Nakshatra Swati Moon

The Rahu Mahadasha does not allow for small living. Rahu placed in the 5th House in Aries in your Vedic chart is a dragon of ambition — hungry for creative expression, spiritual authority, and the kind of recognition that comes from genuine originality. During the Rahu-Venus antardasha now in full force through November 2026, Venus operates as both the vessel and the challenger for that hunger. Your natal Venus sits in Cancer in the 8th House in Ashlesha nakshatra — the nakshatra of the serpent's wisdom, of hidden power, of things that coil before they strike. This is not a gentle Venus. She holds secrets. She knows the transformational potential in intimacy. Today, with the sidereal Sun having just moved into Gemini's final degrees and the transiting Moon traversing Libra — your natal Moon's own sign in the 11th House — the field of your desires and your social networks is electrically active.

Your natal Moon in Swati nakshatra in the 11th House is ruled by Rahu — meaning the Moon itself carries Rahu's restless, independent, wind-swept quality. Swati is the nakshatra of the single blade of grass bending in the storm without breaking: it is the nakshatra of autonomy, commerce, and the cultivation of one's own path apart from inherited structures. Today, with the transiting Moon activating your natal 11th House sector, your circle of influence — your community of aligned souls — comes into focus. Who are the people who are genuinely building with you, and who are merely orbiting? Rahu in the 5th asks this question with particular urgency during this antardasha. The nodes are destiny shapers, and they do not permit indecision about who deserves your creative fire.

The Sagittarius Ascendant gives you Jupiter as your chart ruler — and natal Jupiter sits in Aries in the 5th House in Bharani nakshatra, the nakshatra of containment and consequence, of carrying something to term. This is deeply aligned with the Rahu-Venus period: Bharani asks what you are willing to gestate fully before releasing it into the world. The entrepreneurial and therapeutic work you carry is not finished. Jupiter in Bharani says: do not birth it prematurely for the sake of Rahu's impatience. The solstice is a natural pause-point in the solar cycle — the dharmic invitation today is to feel the fullness of what you are holding without yet releasing it into action. Breathe it. Know it. The right moment will declare itself.
