You are inside Rahu Mahadasha, and the antardasha belongs to Venus through November 2026. In your Vedic chart, Rahu sits in the 5th house in Aries — the house of purva punya, past-life merit, creative intelligence, and the children of the mind. Rahu there is an insatiable force: it amplifies desire around creative output, recognition, and the transmission of knowledge. Venus occupies your 8th house in Cancer, the house of transformation, hidden resources, occult knowledge, and the life-force that moves through the subterranean channels of intimacy and crisis. The Rahu-Venus period is not comfortable by design. It presses the surface-level Rahu appetite against the 8th house depths where Venus operates — you want brilliance, recognition, creative fire, and you keep getting handed the slower medicine of transformation.

Today's transiting Moon moves through sidereal Sagittarius in your 1st house, ruled by Jupiter. Your natal Moon in Swati nakshatra (Libra, 11th house) is ruled by Rahu; this entire Mahadasha is an activation of that Swati quality: independence, movement through open space, the ability to bend without breaking. Swati is symbolized by the wind — it seeks no fixed point, travels through gaps, and carries seeds. Rahu's amplification of this quality during its own Mahadasha means you are in a phase of wide dispersal — ideas, influence, connections spreading outward in ways you cannot fully track or control.

Mercury's Western station retrograde today corresponds in the sidereal frame to Mercury retrograding through Gemini, your 7th house — the house of partnerships, contracts, and the dharma expressed through one-on-one encounter. Mercury rules Gemini and is therefore doubly dignified there, but in retrograde its strength turns inward. Your natal Sun and Mercury are both in Gemini in the 7th under Ardra nakshatra, ruled by Rahu. Today, with Mercury stationing retrograde in the very nakshatra your natal planets occupy, and with Rahu as both Mahadasha lord and Ardra's ruling graha, the sky is pressing your entire chart into the same territory: the past wants to speak. Give it the floor.
