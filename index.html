Rahu, the shadow-graha of insatiable becoming, rules this Mahadasha with its eye fixed on expansion beyond what is familiar. In the 5th house of Aries in the sidereal chart, Rahu's hunger is pointed at creative authority, speculative ventures, and the self that performs in public. Venus, the antardasha lord, sits in the 8th house of Cancer — a placement of concealed wealth, psychological depth, and the sacred terrain of transformation through intimacy. Rahu desires the visible; Venus in the 8th carries what is hidden. The entire dasha period through November 2026 is asking what you are willing to bring out of the 8th house chamber and into the 5th house arena. That emergence is not a crisis — it is the appointed task of this karmic window.

The natal Moon in Swati nakshatra in the 11th house holds the balance point of this configuration. Swati is ruled by Rahu and carries the wind — it balances, sways, and seeks integration across opposing forces. With Ketu also placed in the 11th in Libra, the collective and social sphere of your life is a site of karmic release, not karmic accumulation. The gains you seek through Rahu's 5th house fire are real; the attachments you let go in the 11th are the currency that funds them. Today's transiting Moon in sidereal Sagittarius moves through your 1st house, lighting the body and dharmic self-expression — a signal to occupy yourself fully in public space rather than receding into the 8th house's interior.

Mercury and the Sun, both placed in Ardra nakshatra in the 7th house of Gemini, make this a chart where the encounter with the other — the client, the partner, the interlocutor — is central to the unfolding of dharma. Ardra is the nakshatra of the storm that clears: its presiding deity is Rudra, the howling wind of dissolution and renewal. In the Rahu-Venus period, what is being dissolved in relationships and professional encounters is the performance layer — the self that meets others strategically rather than essentially. The invitation arriving with force in this season is to be met, not merely seen.
