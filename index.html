VEDIC READING

Rahu's Hunger, Venus's Veil

SAG ASC GEMINI SUN · 7H LIBRA MOON · 11H RAHU MAHADASHA VENUS ANTARDASHA RAHU 5H ARIES VENUS 8H CANCER

The grahas do not speak in whispers today — they speak in the language of accumulated dharma. You are in Rahu Mahadasha, and the sub-period of Venus runs through November of this year, a combination that has been accelerating the hidden into the visible with unusual velocity. Rahu sits in your fifth house in Aries by Vedic reckoning — the house of purva punya, of past-life merit, of creative intelligence, of the heart's desire to express itself outward into the world. Rahu in this house amplifies ambition in the domains of creativity, counsel, and love; it makes the appetite for recognition in these areas both a gift and a test. The test is whether you pursue recognition because it confirms your worth, or because your work genuinely serves.

Venus, your antardasha lord, sits in the eighth house in Cancer in your Vedic chart — the house of transformation, of what is concealed, of inheritance both material and spiritual. Cancer Venus in the eighth is not a comfortable placement by Jyotish convention, but it is a profoundly meaningful one: it means your capacity for love and beauty is forged in the hidden places, in the work that happens below the surface of your visible life. During this antardasha, Venus is drawing the transformative material of the eighth house into the light — old wounds around worthiness, the erotic and creative energies that have been sequestered, the financial and relational patterns that belong to a self you are outgrowing. The transit of the Moon through sidereal Sagittarius today activates your first house, conjuncting your Vedic Ascendant and asking you to meet this emergence with an open posture rather than a defended one.

Today's Moon moves through the nakshatra of Mula in the final degrees of Sagittarius — the nakshatra of uprooting, of going to the root of things, governed by Nirriti, the goddess of dissolution. For a Sagittarius Ascendant, the Moon in Mula transiting the first house is a deeply karmic alignment: it asks you to dissolve the identity-structures that were built for survival and have not yet been updated for the life you are actually living. Ketu in your eleventh house in Libra holds the memory of past-life mastery in collective work and social harmony — but it tends toward detachment from the very communities that would recognize your gifts. Today's lunar quality invites you to go to the root of that detachment: not to force it open, but to understand what it is protecting, and whether the protection is still necessary.
